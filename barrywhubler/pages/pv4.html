Grocery VR

Overview

The Grocery VR project was a project that was made for the Boise School District to teach students how to navigate a grocery store and gather the items on a list, and how to pay for the groceries with cash or card. The project was picked up from a previous team’s efforts. Our team was given a layout for the store and a large variety of assets.

Contribution

One of the major problems with the original project was that it was not efficient. The models all had very high polygon counts, textures were measured in gigabytes, and most of the models were using transparency effects. This all led to a very low framerate which can lead to an unsatisfying experience in Virtual Reality. So one of the jobs that I ended up doing on the project was to ensure that the models had efficient geometry and textures. I recreated efficient versions of some of the models, with textures myself, and everytime one of the team members submitted a model, I would check to make sure the model did not have unnecessary geometry, and that the textures were small. If anything was too taxing on the hardware I would either cut it down myself or ask the team member if they could make it more efficient. I created a “shelf stocker” class that would allow anyone to assign products to a shelf then choose the quantity and spacing of the products, so we would not have to assign a position for each product individually. It also deactivates the collision when the user is out of reach of the shelf. I also created interactive doors for the refrigerators, A fruit spawner that spawns fruit in your hand when you reach into a pile of fruit, and fixed various bugs throughout the project.
